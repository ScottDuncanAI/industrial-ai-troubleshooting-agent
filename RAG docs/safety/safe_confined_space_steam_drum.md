# Confined Space Entry Procedure: Steam Drum (SD-1)

**Document No.:** ZJP-SAFE-004  
**Equipment:** Steam Drum SD-1  
**Revision:** 2.1 | **Date:** 2021-08-15  
**Regulatory Basis:** AQ 3028-2008 (Confined Space Safety Requirements in Chemical Industry)

> **CRITICAL:** The steam drum is a confined space as defined by AQ 3028-2008. Entry without a valid confined space permit and full atmospheric testing is PROHIBITED and constitutes a serious safety violation.

---

## 1. Confined Space Hazards

| Hazard | Source | Risk |
|--------|--------|------|
| Oxygen deficiency | CO2 from cooling reactions, displacement by nitrogen inerts | Asphyxiation without warning |
| Carbon monoxide | Residual combustion products in steam/water circuit | Toxic — 200 ppm causes headache/dizziness; 1,600 ppm = fatal in 2 hours |
| Steam / hot surfaces | Residual heat after shutdown | Scalding — wait minimum 36 hours after shutdown |
| Engulfment | Water filling if isolation fails | Drowning |
| Confined geometry | Drum is 1.6 m ID, 8.2 m long — limited egress | Entrapment |

---

## 2. Prerequisites Before Entry Permit Can Be Issued

- [ ] Boiler fully shut down per ZJP-SOP-003 — confirmed stopped > 36 hours
- [ ] PTCA_8322A reads 0 kPa (fully depressurized)
- [ ] All steam and feedwater isolations complete (main steam valve closed, feedwater valve closed, drain valves open)
- [ ] Steam drum temperature confirmed < 50°C (IR gun on drum shell exterior)
- [ ] Both manways open for minimum 4 hours for passive ventilation before gas test
- [ ] Forced ventilation fan installed and running (minimum 10 air changes per hour inside drum)
- [ ] Rescue team briefed and on standby (minimum 2 trained rescuers with SCBA, not just standby — physically present at drum)
- [ ] Emergency contact numbers posted at drum manway

---

## 3. Atmospheric Testing

All testing performed by qualified confined space tester with calibrated multi-gas detector:

| Atmosphere | Required Reading | Action if Not Met |
|------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Oxygen | 19.5–23.5% | Do not enter — continue ventilation; retest after 30 min |
| CO | < 25 ppm | Do not enter — continue ventilation; investigate CO source |
| H2S | < 5 ppm | Do not enter |
| Combustible gas | < 10% LEL | Do not enter |

Test must be performed at:
- Manway level (top of drum)
- Mid-drum (approximately 4 m from each end)
- Bottom of drum (use extension probe through manway)

Record all readings on permit form.

---

## 4. Confined Space Entry Permit Requirements

The permit must specify:
- Name and ID of entrant(s) — maximum 2 entrants simultaneously
- Name of standby/attendant (always outside drum, never enters)
- Name of rescue team members on standby
- Duration of entry authorization (maximum 4 hours per permit — must retest if exceeded)
- Work to be performed inside drum
- Communication method (verbal or radio — maintain continuous communication)
- Rescue retrieval system: entrants must wear full body harness connected to lifeline at all times

Permit must be signed by:
- Entrant(s)
- Supervisor
- Safety Officer
- Permit Issuer (usually maintenance manager)

---

## 5. During Entry

- Continuous gas monitoring: entrant carries personal multi-gas detector clipped to collar
- Standby monitors gas detector readings and maintains communication every 5 minutes
- If gas detector alarms OR communication is lost: standby immediately initiates rescue — do not enter drum without SCBA
- Maximum continuous stay inside drum: 60 minutes (fatigue and heat accumulation)
- Work tools: non-sparking (copper beryllium) if any residual combustible gas detected

---

## 6. Emergency Rescue

If entrant becomes incapacitated:
1. Standby calls for rescue — does NOT enter without SCBA
2. Rescue team dons SCBA and enters
3. Use retrieval line to extract incapacitated entrant — do not drag around internal fixtures
4. Emergency services called simultaneously (dial 119)
5. Administer first aid / CPR if required until EMS arrives

---

## 7. Permit Closure

After work is complete:
- All entrants sign out — confirm all personnel out and all tools removed (tool count)
- Standby signs permit closed
- Permit returned to Safety Officer for filing (retained 3 years)
- Manway closed — new gaskets installed — torqued per ZJP-MNT-010
