# Fly Ash and Bottom Ash Handling and Disposal

**Document No.:** ZJP-ENV-002  
**Revision:** 1.3 | **Date:** 2021-09-01  
**Regulatory Basis:** GB 18599-2020 (General Solid Waste Disposal), GB 51180-2016 (Coal Ash)

---

## 1. Ash Types and Sources

| Ash Type | Source | Quantity (at full load) | Characteristics |
|----------|--------|------------------------|----------------|
| Bottom ash / bed ash | Furnace lower hearth bottom discharge | 2–4 t/h | Coarser particles; higher unburned carbon; may be hot (300–400°C at discharge) |
| Cyclone ash | Cyclone separator diplegs | 15–25 t/h (recirculates — net to collection: minimal) | Fine particles, mostly carbon-depleted |
| Fly ash | Baghouse / economizer hoppers | 1–3 t/h | Very fine particles (< 100 µm); may contain trace heavy metals |

---

## 2. Bottom Ash Handling

### 2.1 Bottom Ash Cooler
Bottom ash discharges continuously from the furnace bed onto the bottom ash cooler (chain grate or rotary drum type). The cooler reduces ash temperature from ~350°C to < 80°C using water-cooled contact.

**Operating checks (every 4 hours):**
- Cooler drive motor running: ☐ Confirmed
- Cooling water supply flow: normal (flowmeter in cooling water circuit)
- No ash bridging at discharge chute (audible sign: cooler motor current rising)
- Bottom ash conveyor operating and discharging to silo

### 2.2 Bottom Ash Silo
- Capacity: 150 tonnes (approximately 3 days at full load)
- Level monitoring: ultrasonic level indicator
- When silo level > 80%: notify logistics for ash truck dispatch
- When silo level > 95%: reduce firing rate until ash removal catches up

---

## 3. Fly Ash Handling

Fly ash from the baghouse and economizer hoppers is collected by pneumatic conveying to the fly ash silo.

- **Fly ash silo capacity:** 200 tonnes
- **Pneumatic conveying air pressure:** 0.4–0.5 MPa
- **Conveying frequency:** Automatic — activated when hopper level switch activates

**Silo full alarm:** If fly ash silo reaches 90%: notify logistics; if no truck available within 4 hours and silo reaches 95%, notify supervisor — reduction in load may be required to reduce ash generation.

---

## 4. Ash Characterization (Annual)

Annual characterization testing per GB 1596 (for potential utilization classification):

| Parameter | Typical Range | Class I Limit (Construction use) |
|-----------|-------------|----------------------------------|
| Loss on ignition (LOI) | 3–8% | < 8% |
| SO₃ content | < 0.5% | < 3% |
| Chloride content | < 0.06% | < 0.06% |
| Heavy metals (As, Pb, Cr, etc.) | Variable | Per GB 5085.3 leach test |

Class I ash (low carbon, low SO₃) can be sold for use in cement, concrete, or road base. Class II ash may require disposal at lined landfill.

---

## 5. Safety Precautions for Ash Handling

| Hazard | Precaution |
|--------|-----------|
| Hot ash (bottom ash) | Do not touch without heat-resistant gloves; confirm cooler operating before any manual handling |
| Silica dust (crystalline) | Respirator (P3 filter) required during any dusty ash handling operation |
| Heavy metals in fly ash | Minimize skin contact; wash hands before eating; annual medical monitoring for fly ash workers |
| Ash silo confined space entry | Full confined space permit required (ZJP-SAFE-004 adapted for silo) |
| Silo overpressure | Silo pressure relief vent must be unobstructed; never seal silo vent during filling |

---

## 6. Ash Disposal Records

Per GB 18599-2020, the plant must maintain records of:
- Monthly ash quantity generated (tonnes)
- Ash characterization test results
- Disposal destination (recycling customer or licensed landfill)
- Transport manifests for each ash truck dispatched

Records retained for 5 years and available for regulatory inspection.
