# Hot Work Permit Procedure

**Document No.:** ZJP-SAFE-005  
**Revision:** 1.5 | **Date:** 2021-08-15  
**Regulatory Basis:** GB 9448-1999 (Welding and Cutting Safety), AQ 3022-2008

> Hot work (welding, cutting, grinding, open flame) in or near the boiler creates ignition sources in the presence of coal dust, fuel oil, and flammable gases. Every hot work operation requires a permit regardless of perceived risk level.

---

## 1. Definition of Hot Work

Hot work includes any operation that produces flame, sparks, or heat sufficient to ignite coal dust or vapors:
- Arc welding and cutting (MMA, MIG, TIG)
- Oxy-acetylene welding and cutting
- Grinding (angle grinder)
- Brazing
- Open-flame heating (torch)
- High-power abrasive blasting that generates sparks

---

## 2. Boiler-Specific Hazards for Hot Work

| Location | Specific Hazard |
|----------|----------------|
| Furnace interior | Residual coal dust on walls and in bed material — combustible |
| Cyclone interior | Residual ash deposits with embedded unburned carbon |
| Coal bunker area | Continuous coal dust accumulation — explosion risk |
| Oil fuel system area | Fuel oil vapors |
| Backpass / economizer | Ash deposits with possible residual carbon |

---

## 3. Pre-Permit Requirements

Before issuing any hot work permit in the boiler area:
- [ ] Area survey: identify all combustibles within 10 m of hot work site
- [ ] Coal bunkers closed/isolated from hot work zone
- [ ] Ash and coal dust removed from hot work area (radius 3 m) — vacuum clean; do not sweep (sweeping raises dust)
- [ ] Combustible gas test: < 10% LEL in work area and in any adjacent closed spaces (e.g., inside furnace if working on furnace wall)
- [ ] Fire watch assigned — trained person with fire extinguisher positioned at work site for entire duration plus 30 minutes after work ends
- [ ] Fire extinguisher: CO2 type (minimum 5 kg), rated for electrical fires (if electrical welding)
- [ ] Water hose connected and pressurized at nearby hose station
- [ ] Boiler status: confirmed shutdown; no live coal burning within furnace

---

## 4. Permit Components

Each hot work permit (Form ZJP-F-005) must specify:
- Location of hot work (specific equipment and exact position)
- Type of hot work (welding, cutting, grinding)
- Start and end time (maximum 8 hours per permit — must reissue for next shift)
- Name of welder/operator performing work
- Name of fire watch
- Gas test result (time, reading, tester signature)
- Fire extinguisher location and type confirmed
- Supervisor sign-off
- Safety Officer sign-off

---

## 5. During Hot Work

- Fire watch must remain at site continuously — no exceptions
- If fire watch must leave: hot work STOPS until fire watch returns
- Gas re-testing: every 2 hours if work continues more than 2 hours
- Sparks/spatter control: use fire blankets on adjacent surfaces
- Welding cable routing: do not route cables through doorways or over sharp edges; do not use furnace shell as ground path

---

## 6. Post-Work Fire Watch

For 30 minutes after hot work stops:
- Fire watch remains at site
- Inspect all surrounding surfaces, crevices, and downwind areas for smoldering ignition
- Only after 30-minute fire watch with no signs of smoldering: permit can be closed

---

## 7. Permit Close-Out

Fire watch signs permit closed with time. Permit retained by Safety Officer for 1 year.

If fire is discovered during or after hot work:
1. Fire watch uses extinguisher — call for help immediately (do not fight alone)
2. Activate fire alarm
3. Notify shift supervisor
4. Complete fire incident report within 24 hours
