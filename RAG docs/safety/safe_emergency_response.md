# Boiler Emergency Response Plan

**Document No.:** ZJP-SAFE-006  
**Revision:** 3.0 | **Date:** 2022-03-01  
**Applicability:** Unit 1 — All Personnel  
**Emergency Contact:** Plant Security/Fire Brigade: 119 | Shift Supervisor: Extension 201 | Plant Doctor: Extension 205

---

## 1. Emergency Types Covered

1. Boiler furnace puff / explosion
2. Steam main rupture
3. IDF catastrophic failure
4. Coal fire / dust explosion
5. Personnel injury in boiler area
6. Fire in boiler house

---

## 2. General Emergency Actions (All Types)

1. **Sound alarm** — pull nearest manual fire alarm pull station OR press BPS emergency stop pushbutton
2. **Call out** — verbally alert all personnel in boiler area to evacuate
3. **Evacuate** — all non-essential personnel to muster point (Car Park A, north gate — minimum 50 m from boiler)
4. **Account for personnel** — shift supervisor performs head count at muster point
5. **Call emergency services** — Fire Brigade: 119; Ambulance: 120 if injuries
6. **Do not re-enter** boiler area until declared safe by emergency coordinator

---

## 3. Furnace Puff / Explosion

**Indicators:** Sudden loud report from furnace, PT_8313A–F going strongly positive, hot gas and ash ejected from access ports or expansion joints.

**Actions:**
1. EVACUATE immediately — all personnel within 30 m of furnace
2. Boiler protection system should have tripped automatically — confirm on DCS if possible from safe location
3. Do NOT open any furnace access doors after event until furnace has cooled and been confirmed safe by emergency coordinator
4. Account for all personnel — check for injuries from blast overpressure or hot gas ejection
5. Post-event: mandatory structural inspection before any restart

---

## 4. Steam Main Rupture

**Indicators:** Loud hissing, steam cloud visible, TE_8332A or PTCA_8324 drops suddenly, ZZQBCHLL drops.

**Actions:**
1. Evacuate zone around steam release — superheated steam at 540°C is immediately lethal
2. From safe location: trip boiler via BPS emergency pushbutton
3. Close main steam isolation valve remotely if DCS allows
4. Account for personnel — steam burns are the priority injury type
5. First aid: cool burn areas with cool (not cold) water for minimum 20 minutes; do not remove clothing stuck to burn

---

## 5. IDF Catastrophic Failure

**Indicators:** Loud metallic sound from IDF area, YFJ3_ZD1 or ZD2 sudden spike, then zero (fan stopped), furnace pressure rising.

**Actions:**
1. BPS should trip boiler automatically
2. Evacuate IDF area immediately — rotating machinery failure can project debris
3. Do not approach IDF until shaft is confirmed stationary (30 minutes after stop)
4. Assess: is ductwork intact? Is there structural damage to IDF casing?
5. Engineering inspection required before any attempt to restart

---

## 6. Coal Dust Explosion / Fire

**Indicators:** Loud report near coal bunker or conveyor area, fire visible, coal dust cloud.

**Actions:**
1. Sound alarm; call Fire Brigade (119) immediately
2. Evacuate coal handling area
3. If safe to do so, shut down coal conveyor and coal feeder from control room
4. Do NOT use water on a coal dust fire initially — use CO2 extinguisher; then water fog when dust has settled
5. Boiler trip if coal supply is interrupted

---

## 7. Personnel Injury

**Actions by First Responders:**
1. Scene safety first — do not approach if area is still hazardous
2. Call for help: Extension 205 (plant doctor) and 120 (ambulance) simultaneously
3. Apply first aid:
   - Burns: cool water for 20 minutes
   - Electrical shock: do NOT touch victim if source not confirmed isolated; use non-conductive tool to separate
   - CO poisoning: move to fresh air; begin CPR if not breathing
4. Do not move injured person with suspected spinal injury
5. Meet ambulance at plant gate and escort to victim

---

## 8. Muster Point and Accountability

**Muster Point:** Car Park A, north gate (GPS: see plant site map ZJP-DWG-SITE-001)  
**Accountability:** Shift supervisor calls roll using crew list — all personnel must be accounted for within 10 minutes of alarm  
**Visitor log:** All visitors signed in must be tracked and accounted for

---

## 9. Post-Emergency Reporting

Within 24 hours of any emergency event:
- Shift supervisor completes incident report (Form ZJP-F-006)
- Preliminary root cause and contributing factors identified
- Regulatory notification: any injury or major equipment damage reportable to provincial safety authority within 4 hours (per Chinese work safety law)
