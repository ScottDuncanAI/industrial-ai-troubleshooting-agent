# Equipment Datasheet — Stack / Chimney

**Document No.:** ZJP-DS-014  
**Equipment Tag:** STK-1  
**P&ID Reference:** P&ID-8300-016  
**Revision:** 1.0 | **Date:** 2020-08-01

---

## 1. Equipment Description

The chimney (exhaust stack) is the final point of discharge for flue gas to the atmosphere after passing through the induced draft fan. It provides sufficient height to ensure atmospheric dispersion of emissions and creates natural draft that supplements the IDF. The chimney has no instrumentation in the standard plant historian but is subject to periodic external inspection and emissions monitoring per environmental regulations.

**System:** Draft & Exhaust System  
**Criticality:** Low (structural failure risk, not process risk)

## 2. Design Specifications

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Height | 80 m |
| Internal Diameter at Base | 3,200 mm |
| Internal Diameter at Top | 2,400 mm |
| Material (inner liner) | Acid-resistant brick lining |
| Material (outer shell) | Reinforced concrete |
| Flue Gas Inlet Temperature | 130–150°C (after air preheaters) |
| Design Flue Gas Flow | 450,000 m³/h (wet) |
| Natural Draft Contribution | −180 Pa (adds to IDF suction) |
| Stack Opacity Monitor | Installed at mid-height |

## 3. Emissions Monitoring

| Pollutant | Regulatory Limit (GB 13271) | Monitoring Method |
|-----------|----------------------------|-------------------|
| SO₂ | 200 mg/m³ | CEMS (online) |
| NOₓ | 200 mg/m³ | CEMS (online) |
| Particulate matter | 30 mg/m³ | CEMS (online) |
| Opacity | < 20% | Transmissometer (online) |
| CO | 80 mg/m³ | Periodic sampling |

Continuous Emissions Monitoring System (CEMS) data is reported to the environmental authority monthly. See ZJP-ENV-001 for operating limits and reporting requirements.

---

## 5. Normal Operating Appearance

- **Normal flue gas color:** Near-invisible to light gray plume; white water vapor plume in cold weather (< 15°C ambient) is normal
- **Black smoke** (opacity > 40%): Indicates serious combustion problem (low O2, high coal, bed disturbance) — report immediately
- **Dense white plume (not cold weather):** May indicate steam leak from heat recovery section entering flue gas path
- **Yellow/brown plume:** Possible indication of high SO₂ or NOₓ — investigate combustion and coal quality
