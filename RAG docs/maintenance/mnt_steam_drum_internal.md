# Maintenance Procedure: Steam Drum Internal Inspection

**Document No.:** ZJP-MNT-010  
**Revision:** 2.0 | **Date:** 2021-06-15  
**Applicability:** Steam Drum SD-1  
**Maintenance Type:** 3-year (mandatory regulatory inspection) / Annual visual if accessible  
**Estimated Duration:** 24 hours (full team, including cooling, entry, and closure)

---

## 1. Scope

The steam drum internal inspection is a mandatory regulatory requirement under Chinese pressure vessel law. It includes visual inspection of all internal surfaces, non-destructive examination (NDE) of selected welds, inspection of steam-water separation internals, and re-inspection of any previously identified defects.

**Regulatory Basis:** GB/T 151, TSG 21-2016 (Boiler Safety Technical Supervision Regulations)  
**Third-Party Inspector Required:** Licensed pressure vessel inspector must witness and sign the inspection report.

---

## 2. Pre-Entry Requirements

- [ ] Drum fully depressurized — PTCA_8322A reads 0 kPa
- [ ] Drum cooled to < 50°C — minimum 36 hours after shutdown
- [ ] Both manways open for at least 4 hours for natural ventilation before entry
- [ ] Gas test: O2 > 19.5%, CO < 25 ppm, H2S < 1 ppm, combustibles < 10% LEL
- [ ] Continuous gas monitoring during all entry
- [ ] Confined space entry permit issued (ZJP-SAFE-004)
- [ ] Inspector's own PPE and safety harness (drum is 1.6 m ID — cramped quarters)
- [ ] Communication established with standby outside

---

## 3. Internal Surface Inspection

### 3.1 Shell (Lower Half — Water Side)
- [ ] Shell plate: tap test for pitting — concentrated pitting in waterline zone (most common corrosion area)
- [ ] Any pitting depth: measure with pit gauge — record on shell inspection map
  - Acceptable: isolated pits < 0.8 mm deep
  - Review: pits 0.8–2.0 mm deep — NDE required
  - Repair: pits > 2.0 mm, or clusters > 10 pits/m² > 0.5 mm — weld repair before restart
- [ ] Shell seam welds (longitudinal and circumferential): visual for cracks, corrosion, or undercut
- [ ] Chemical dosing nozzle area: pitting or preferential corrosion around nozzle (turbulent injection zone)

### 3.2 Shell (Upper Half — Steam Side)
- [ ] Steam space: any carryover deposits (white crystalline scale = sodium silicate/phosphate carryover — indicates water chemistry issue)
- [ ] Manway flange seating surface: no pitting or scratches — affects seal integrity

### 3.3 Nozzle Connections (All)
| Nozzle | Check |
|--------|-------|
| Downcomers (water side) | Erosion at inlet, no blockage |
| Risers (steam/water mixture) | No blockage, no debris |
| Feedwater inlet | No impingement erosion on opposite shell plate |
| Safety valve nozzles | Clean seats, no corrosion on valve body or nozzle neck |
| Chemical dosing | Internal pipe condition (may have drilled holes — confirm not blocked) |
| Level transmitter nozzles | Clear, no scale buildup |

---

## 4. Steam-Water Separation Internals Inspection

### 4.1 Primary Separators (24 Cyclone Units)
- [ ] Cyclone bodies: no cracks, no corrosion damage
- [ ] Cyclone mounting brackets: secure, no loose fasteners
- [ ] Cyclone inlet slot: clear, no scale bridging

### 4.2 Secondary Separators (Chevron Demister)
- [ ] Wire mesh intact — no missing sections or crushing damage
- [ ] Demister frame: properly seated, no bypass gaps at edges
- [ ] Scale/deposits on mesh: flush with clean water if heavily fouled

### 4.3 Feedwater Distribution Pipe
- [ ] Pipe internal condition: no blockage
- [ ] Distribution holes: all open (confirm with light inspection or compressed air test)
- [ ] Thermal sleeve at feedwater inlet nozzle: intact (protects drum shell from thermal shock)

---

## 5. NDE Requirements

Per regulatory requirements, the following NDE is performed at every 3-year inspection:

| Inspection Item | Method | Coverage |
|----------------|--------|----------|
| Shell longitudinal seams | UT or radiography | 100% of seam length |
| Shell circumferential seams | Magnetic particle testing (MT) | 100% |
| Nozzle-to-shell welds | MT or PT | 100% |
| Confirmed pit areas (from visual) | UT thickness survey | 100% of flagged areas |

All NDE must be performed by Level II or Level III certified technicians. NDE reports attached to inspection record.

---

## 6. Safety Valve Test

Annual safety valve testing is performed as part of the drum inspection outage:
1. SV-1 (set point 10.78 MPa) — lift test at test pressure
2. SV-2 (set point 10.88 MPa) — lift test at test pressure
3. Confirm valves reseat cleanly after lift — no leakage

---

## 7. Post-Inspection Closure

1. Remove all tools, equipment, rags from drum interior (tool count-in and count-out mandatory)
2. Install new manway gaskets (spiral-wound, graphite-filled, rated 12 MPa)
3. Torque manway bolts to 280 Nm in cross-pattern — 3 passes
4. Fill drum with treated water — monitor for leakage during fill
5. Regulatory inspector's final walkdown and sign-off before pressurization
