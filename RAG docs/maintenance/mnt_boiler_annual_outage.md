# Annual Outage Master Checklist

**Document No.:** ZJP-MNT-013  
**Revision:** 3.1 | **Date:** 2022-01-10  
**Applicability:** Unit 1 — All Equipment  
**Planned Duration:** 14 days (target — adjust based on findings)

---

## 1. Purpose

This master checklist coordinates all work orders, inspections, and third-party visits during the annual boiler outage. Each task references the applicable procedure. The outage coordinator (Engineering Manager or designee) owns this document and is responsible for sequencing and sign-off.

---

## 2. Pre-Outage Preparation (2 Weeks Before)

- [ ] All work orders issued and approved — maintenance management system updated
- [ ] Spare parts on-site: bearings, refractory materials, gaskets, superheater tube plugs
- [ ] Third-party inspectors booked: pressure vessel inspector, NDE technician, alignment specialist
- [ ] LOTO isolation register prepared and pre-signed by shift supervisor
- [ ] Scaffold erection plan approved (furnace interior, cyclone interior, backpass)

## 4. Critical Path

The critical path for this outage is typically:
1. Boiler cool-down (24 hours)
2. Cyclone refractory inspection and repair (up to 5 days including cure)
3. Steam drum inspection (3 days including NDE)
4. Regulatory sign-off
5. Boiler pressurization and hot restart

All other work must be completed and cleared **before** steam drum is sealed for pressurization.

---

## 5. Return-to-Service Checklist

Before authorizing restart:
- [ ] All work orders closed or formally deferred
- [ ] LOTO isolation register: all devices removed and signed off
- [ ] Third-party inspector sign-off received (if 3-year drum inspection)
- [ ] All instrumentation calibration certificates received
- [ ] Refractory cure time completed (if furnace or cyclone repairs performed)
- [ ] Hydrotest complete and passed (if required)
- [ ] Pre-startup safety inspection (ZJP-SOP-007) completed
- [ ] Plant manager approval for restart
