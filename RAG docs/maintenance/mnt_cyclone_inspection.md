# Maintenance Procedure: Cyclone Separator Internal Inspection

**Document No.:** ZJP-MNT-008  
**Revision:** 1.6 | **Date:** 2021-06-01  
**Applicability:** Left Cyclone Separator (CS-L) and Right Cyclone Separator (CS-R)  
**Maintenance Type:** Annual Outage — MANDATORY  
**Estimated Duration:** 16 hours each (3-person crew)

---

## 1. Scope

Cyclone separators experience the most severe service conditions of any equipment in the boiler — high temperature (850–900°C), high-velocity abrasive solids, and continuous thermal cycling. Refractory failure here directly leads to loss of solids separation efficiency and rapid bed inventory loss. This inspection is mandatory at every annual outage.

---

## 2. Isolation and Entry Preparation

- [ ] Boiler shutdown, fully depressurized, and LOTO complete
- [ ] Cyclone cooled to < 50°C — minimum 24 hours after shutdown with draft maintained
- [ ] Confined space entry permit issued per ZJP-SAFE-004 (cyclone interior is a confined space)
- [ ] Gas test performed: O2 > 19.5%, CO < 50 ppm, combustible < 5% LEL
- [ ] Continuous gas monitoring required during entry
- [ ] Rescue team on standby if entry depth > 1.5 m

---

## 3. Pre-Entry Inspection (Endoscope)

Before entry, perform borescope inspection through all available access ports (typically 4 per cyclone):
- [ ] Vortex finder: visible erosion, crack lines, missing sections
- [ ] Inlet duct: refractory condition at leading edge (highest erosion zone)
- [ ] Lower cone: ash accumulation or blockage in outlet

---

## 4. Internal Inspection Sequence

Inspection team: minimum 2 persons (one in cyclone, one outside as standby/attendant).

### 4.1 Inlet Duct (Highest Priority — Most Erosion)
- [ ] Inlet leading edge refractory: measure remaining thickness with calibrated probe in minimum 20 spots
  - Minimum acceptable: 80 mm (design: 200 mm)
  - Alert: 100–130 mm — plan repair at next opportunity
  - Critical: < 80 mm — repair mandatory before restart
- [ ] Inlet duct walls: no exposed membrane steel visible through refractory
- [ ] Keying (anchor system): refractory keys intact — tap test to identify hollow areas

### 4.2 Cyclone Body
- [ ] Inner wall refractory thickness: probe check at 1 m elevation intervals
  - Minimum acceptable: 60 mm (design: 150 mm)
- [ ] Tap test entire inner surface: hollow sound indicates delaminated refractory — must be repaired
- [ ] Circumferential cracks: acceptable if < 5 mm wide and < 500 mm long; > 5 mm wide requires repair before restart
- [ ] Casing steel (exposed at any refractory gaps): record location and size — emergency repair required

### 4.3 Vortex Finder (Most Critical Component)
- [ ] Remaining thickness: measure at lip (bottom 300 mm of vortex finder)
  - Minimum acceptable: 50 mm (design: 100 mm high-chrome castable)
  - Replace if < 60 mm — replacement takes 48 hours of casting and cure
- [ ] Lip geometry: should be smooth, circular — eroded or irregular lip reduces separation efficiency significantly
- [ ] Cracks: no axial cracks deeper than 20 mm

### 4.4 Lower Cone and Dipleg
- [ ] Cone refractory: thickness check at 500 mm intervals — minimum 60 mm
- [ ] Dipleg outlet: clear, no bridged ash or refractory debris
- [ ] L-seal refractory: check at fluidizing port area
- [ ] FT_8306A/B ports: clear (use compressed air purge if any indication of restriction)

---

## 5. Refractory Repair (If Required)

Refractory repairs use the same material as original construction — mixing and casting per supplier specification (Harbison-Walker RESCO AA-22S or equivalent):
- Anchor density minimum 30 per m² (V-type anchors, 316SS)
- Curing time: 24 hours at ambient + 8-hour controlled heat-up per curing curve
- No water spray during cure
- Record repair locations on cyclone inspection sketch (attach to maintenance report)

---

## 6. Differential Pressure Transmitter Verification (ZCLCCY, YCLCCY)

With cyclone cooled and static:
1. Confirm impulse lines are clear — blow out with nitrogen if any indication of blockage
2. Zero check: with zero flow, should read near 0 Pa at ambient
3. Confirm instrument range and alarm setpoints match ZJP-CTRL-004
