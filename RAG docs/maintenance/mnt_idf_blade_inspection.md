# Maintenance Procedure: IDF Fan Blade and Impeller Inspection

**Document No.:** ZJP-MNT-004  
**Revision:** 1.4 | **Date:** 2021-05-10  
**Applicability:** Unit 1 Induced Draft Fan (YFJ3)  
**Maintenance Type:** Planned — Annual Outage (performed concurrently with ZJP-MNT-003)  
**Estimated Duration:** 4 hours (in addition to bearing work)

---

## 1. Scope

Fly ash passing through the IDF impeller is abrasive and causes progressive erosion of fan blades. This procedure covers visual and dimensional inspection of the impeller to detect erosion, imbalance risk, and any structural damage before they cause catastrophic failure or unacceptable vibration.

---

## 2. Safety Requirements

- IDF must be fully LOTO per ZJP-SAFE-001 before any work inside the casing
- Allow impeller to come to complete rest — wait minimum 30 minutes after last rotation
- Confirm with contact tachometer (not by touch) that shaft is stationary
- Confined space entry not required for blade inspection via access port; full entry requires permit (ZJP-SAFE-004)

---

## 3. Inspection Access

The IDF casing has inspection ports on the inlet cone (×2) and casing body (×4). Remove inspection port covers (typically 8 × M12 bolts each). Use flashlight and mirror or borescope camera for full blade coverage.

---

## 4. Blade Erosion Inspection

Record the following for each of the 12 blades on both impellers (double-inlet fan):

### 4.1 Visual Assessment
- [ ] Blade leading edge: sharp (new) / rounded (moderate wear) / significantly eroded (replace)
- [ ] Blade tip: no cracks, no bent tip
- [ ] Blade pressure face: no deep gouging or pitting
- [ ] Blade attachment welds to hub: no visible cracking

### 4.2 Thickness Measurement (Ultrasonic)
Measure blade thickness at leading edge, midspan, and trailing edge for 3 representative blades (select blades showing most visible wear). Record on form ZJP-F-003.

| Location | Design Thickness | Minimum Acceptable | Measured |
|----------|-----------------|-------------------|---------|
| Leading edge | 14 mm | 8 mm | _______ mm |
| Mid-span | 12 mm | 7 mm | _______ mm |
| Trailing edge | 10 mm | 6 mm | _______ mm |

If any measurement is below the minimum acceptable: blade set replacement is mandatory.

---

## 5. Impeller Balance Assessment

If bearings were found to be in acceptable condition but vibration was elevated before shutdown (YFJ3_ZD1 or YFJ3_ZD2 > 3.5 mm/s), blade erosion-induced imbalance is the most likely cause.

**Acceptable remaining imbalance at operating speed:** < 2.5 g·m per plane (ISO 1940-1, Balance Grade G6.3)

If impeller weight reduction from erosion is estimated > 500 g per blade (based on UT measurements), send impeller to vendor for re-balancing.

---

## 6. Erosion Protection Measures

Chromium carbide hard-facing applied to leading edges extends blade life from ~8,000 hours to ~18,000 hours. If hard-facing is present, inspect for delamination or spalling of the overlay.

---

## 7. Acceptance Criteria Summary

| Finding | Action |
|---------|--------|
| Uniform erosion, all blades within minimum thickness | Return to service — monitor vibration |
| Any blade below minimum thickness | Replace blade set before restart |
| Blade crack at root or weld | Immediate replacement — do not return to service |
| Hard-facing delamination > 20% of blade area | Re-apply hard-facing before restart |
| Post-reassembly vibration > 2.5 mm/s after balancing attempt | Re-balance by specialist |
